May, 2008

PRESIDENT SIGNS GENETIC INFORMATION NONDISCRIMINATION ACT

On May 21, 2008, President Bush signed H.R. 493, the Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act (GINA) of 2008. This new law prohibits discrimination on the basis of genetic information with respect to health insurance and employment.

GINA prohibits health plans, insurers and employers from requesting, requiring or purchasing the results of genetic tests and also forbids them from disclosing genetic information. The new law also prohibits employers from firing, refusing to hire or otherwise discriminating against employees with respect to compensation and other terms of employment.

GINA is not expected to have a very big impact. The Congressional Budget Office estimated that it would increase the number of individuals who obtain health insurance by about 600 people per year, nearly all of whom would obtain insurance in the individual market.

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 already prohibits group health plans from using genetic information to deny coverage or adjust premium rates of an individual within a group. GINA prohibits group health plans and group insurers from adjusting premiums or contributions for a group based on genetic information of an individual enrolled in a plan.

Employers are allowed to offer health and genetic services to an individual as part of a wellness program if:

  • The individual provides prior, knowing, voluntary and written authorization
  • Only the individual (or family member if they receive the services) and a health care professional involved in providing services receives individually identifiable information, and
  • The employer receives only aggregated, de-identified information.

    • The GINA provisions that apply to group health plans will be effective for plan years beginning on and after May 21, 2009. The GINA provisions that apply to employers (including provisions related to wellness plans) will be effective on November 21, 2009.

    GINA does not pre-empt state laws that provide greater protection.

    CONGRESS PASSES BILL TO ALLOW FSA DISTRIBUTIONS FOR RESERVISTS

    On May 22, 2008, the US Senate passed H.R. 6081, the Heroes Earnings Assistance and Relief Tax Act of 2008. The House passed it on May 20, 2008. Having passed in identical form in both the House and Senate, this bill now awaits the signature of the President before becoming law.

    Among other things, this bill allows employers to amend their flexible spending accounts to permit reservists called up for at least 6 months of active duty to take FSA balances as a taxable distribution in cash.

    CALIFORNIA SUPREME COURT OKs SAME-SEX MARRIAGES

    On May 15, 2008, the California Supreme Court ruled that it is unconstitutional to deprive gays and lesbians of the equal right to marriage. The ruling should have minimal impact on insured plans in California since they are already required to cover domestic partners registered with the state.

    Opponents of same-sex marriage have already qualified an initiative for the November ballot that would amend the state constitution to say that marriage is only between a man and a woman.
