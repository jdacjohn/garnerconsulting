November, 2004

IRS Releases 2005 Plan Limits

The Internal Revenue Service has announced various annual changes to indexed amounts for a variety of plans. The changes include:

  • Single coverage deductibles for Archer Medical Savings Accounts (MSAs) can be $1,750 to $2,650 (with out-of-pocket costs to be no more than $3,500) and family coverage deductibles for Archer MSAs will be $3,500 to $5,250 (with out-of-pocket costs to be no more than $6,450).
  • Minimum deductibles for health savings accounts (HSAs) are unchanged at $1,000 for single coverage and $2,000 for family coverage. Maximum out-of-pocket limits increase from $5,000 to $5,100 for single coverage and from $10,000 to $10,200 for family coverage. Maximum contributions are tied to the Archer MSA deductible limits, which will be increased from $2,600 to $2,650 for single coverage and from $5,150 to $5,250 for a family.
  • The per diem limit for tax-free long-term care benefits has been raised from $230 to $240 per day.
  • Section 416(i) - the compensation limit used to determine who is a key employee will increase from $130,000 to $135,000.
  • Section 414(q) - compensation used in the definition of highly compensated will increase from $90,000 to $95,000.
  • The annual compensation limit for benefits provided through Voluntary Employees’ Beneficiary Associations will increase from $205,000 to $210,000.
  • Mileage rate changes include an increase from 37.5 cents per mile to 40.5 cents in 2005 for business mileage and from 14 cents to 15 cents per mile for medical expense deduction purposes.
  • The monthly parking limit has been raised from $195 to $200, while mass transit and vanpooling has been increased from $100 to $105.
  • The adoption tax credit is increasing from $10,390 to $10,630.

Medicare Changes Announced

The Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) has announced the annual Medicare changes for 2005, which include the following:

  • The inpatient hospital deductible will be $912 for the first 60 days, up from $876 in 2004;
  • The daily coinsurance amount for the 61st through 90th days of hospitalization in a benefit period will be $228 (1/4 of the inpatient hospital deductible), up from $219 in 2004;
  • The daily coinsurance amount for lifetime reserve days will be $456 (1/2 of the inpatient hospital deductible), up from $438 in 2004;
  • The daily coinsurance amount for the 21st through 100th days of extended care services in a skilled nursing facility in a benefit period will be $114 (1/8 of the inpatient hospital deductible), up from $109.50;
  • The Part A monthly premium will be $375, up from $343 in 2004 for a person who has fewer than 30 quarters of Medicare-covered employment and $206, up from $189 in 2004, for a person who has 30 to 39 quarters of Medicare-covered employment;
  • The Part B monthly premium will be $78.20, up from $66.60 in 2004.

California Voters Repeal Pay-Or-Play Law

In the November elections, California’s voters repealed, by the narrow margin of 50.9% to 49.1%, last year’s new law (SB 2), which would have required large employers (200 or more employees) to pay 80% of both employee and dependent premiums beginning January 1, 2006 or pay a fee to a fund that would provide health insurance to those employees and dependents. Employers with 50 through 199 employees would have been required to pay 80% of employee premiums beginning January 1, 2007 or pay a fee. Employers with 20 through 49 employees would not have been required to do anything until the legislature approved a subsidy. Employers with less than 20 employees were exempt.
